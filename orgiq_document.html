OrgIQ Overview
What is this project

What OrgIQ Does

OrgIQ is a real-time HR analytics platform built on AWS. It turns raw employee data sitting in a database into actionable charts, risk alerts, and org structure — all on one secure, role-gated dashboard that HR and leadership can open in any browser.

The Problem It Solves

HR teams sit on piles of data spread across spreadsheets and siloed systems. OrgIQ aggregates everything nightly, computes the metrics that matter, and presents them on one screen with no manual work.

Who It Is For

HR administrators and leadership who need a quick pulse on workforce health — who might leave, how hiring is going, which departments are burning through leave — without digging through raw data.

OrgIQ Technology
Every service and why it was chosen

Technology Stack

Service AWS Product What it does in this project
DatabaseDynamoDBStores raw HR data in 5 tables (employees, leave, performance, recruitment, analytics). Serverless NoSQL — no schema to manage, scales automatically, pay-per-request pricing.
ComputeLambda (×4)Python functions that run on demand. The aggregation Lambda fires on a schedule; the API Lambdas fire on each dashboard request. No servers to maintain.
SchedulerEventBridgeTriggers the aggregation Lambda every day at 2 AM UTC using a cron expression. Fully managed — no cron jobs, no EC2 instance needed.
APIAPI GatewayExposes three endpoints: /metrics for all chart data, /org-chart for the reporting tree, /health for CloudWatch stats. Handles routing, CORS, and throttling.
AuthCognitoManages login. The browser calls Cognito directly — no server in between. Returns a JWT token on success. Enforces HR_Admin and Leadership group membership before granting access.
HostingS3 + WebsiteThe entire dashboard is one HTML file served from an S3 bucket configured as a static website. No web server, no maintenance, ~$0.00/month hosting cost.
MonitoringCloudWatchStores Lambda invocation counts, error rates, and API Gateway request volume automatically. The /health Lambda reads these in real time for the System Health tab.
ChartsChart.js + D3Chart.js renders bar, stacked bar, doughnut, and line charts from CDN. D3.js v7 renders the interactive org chart tree with zoom, pan, and tooltips.

How Attrition Risk Is Calculated

score = (0.35 × leave_ratio) + (0.35 × perf_inverse) + (0.20 × tenure_risk) + (0.10 × absence_freq)

Overview Tab & Headcount Tab

Step 3 — Overview Tab
What is this

The first screen after login. It makes one API call to /metrics?type=all and renders everything from the pre-computed analytics DynamoDB table — numbers the nightly Lambda already calculated.

What to do

Look at the five KPI cards at the top. Then scroll down to see the four charts below them.

Total headcount (66), high-risk employees (2), avg leave utilisation (175%), offer accept rate (69%). Below: headcount bars per department, attrition risk doughnut, leave utilisation grouped bars, and the recruitment funnel.

Step 4 — Headcount Tab
What is this

Three charts showing workforce size over time. The hires vs departures chart covers the last 6 months per department. Useful for spotting whether teams are growing or shrinking and when.

What to do

Click Headcount in the top navigation bar.

What you see

Three charts load: monthly hires vs departures as a grouped bar chart, hires per department stacked by month, and current headcount split as a doughnut. Each chart is interactive — hover for exact numbers.

OrgIQ Demo Steps 5 – 6 of 10

Leave Tab & Recruitment Tab

Step 5 — Leave Tab
What is this

Shows how much of each department's monthly leave quota has been consumed. A number over 100% means the team used more than their monthly allocation — this sometimes signals burnout or understaffing.

What to do

Click Leave in the navigation bar.

What you see

A grouped bar chart comparing current month vs 3-month average for each department. Below it, individual department cards show the exact percentage with a colour indicator — red over 90%, amber over 60%, blue below.

Step 6 — Recruitment Tab
What is this

Tracks 120 applicants through five pipeline stages. Uses cumulative counts — so each bar shows everyone who reached at least that stage, making the funnel shape correct (each bar is always smaller than the one above).

What to do

Click Recruitment in the navigation bar.

What you see

Horizontal funnel bars on the left. On the right: four conversion rates — shortlist rate, interview rate, offer rate, join rate. Below, a bar chart shows the absolute drop-off from stage to stage.

OrgIQ Demo Steps 7 – 8 of 10

Attrition Risk Tab & Org Chart Tab

Step 7 — Attrition Risk Tab
What is this

Every active employee gets a risk score from 0 to 100 every night. The table shows the top 10 highest-scoring people. Score is based on 4 factors: leave usage (35%), performance score inverted (35%), tenure shape (20%), absence frequency (10%).

What to do

Click Attrition Risk in the navigation. Then try clicking any column header to sort the table.

What you see

A sortable table of 10 employees. Each row has a coloured progress bar (red = high risk, amber = medium, green = low), plus department, role, leave days, performance score, and tenure in months.

Step 8 — Org Chart Tab
What is this

Built by reading the manager_id field on every employee record in real time. The Lambda traces those relationships recursively to build a tree starting at the CEO. D3.js renders it as a live interactive diagram.

What to do

Click Org Chart in the navigation. Use scroll to zoom in and out. Click and drag to pan. Hover over any node.

What you see

A tree of coloured nodes — each one is an employee, colour matching their department. Hovering shows their name, role, department, and tenure. The full hierarchy from CEO to individual contributors is visible.

OrgIQ Demo Steps 9 – 10 of 10

System Health Tab & Sign Out

Step 9 — System Health Tab
What is this

The /health Lambda calls the real CloudWatch API to fetch actual operational metrics — not simulated data. It reads API Gateway request counts, Lambda invocation counts, error rates, and P99 latency from the last 24 hours.

What to do

Click System Health in the navigation. Wait 2–3 seconds while the Lambda fetches from CloudWatch.

What you see

A grid of real metric cards: API requests (24h), Lambda invocations, error rate %, P99 latency, DynamoDB read units, last aggregation time. Below: two line charts showing hourly API request volume and Lambda error count over the last 24 hours. A function-by-function breakdown shows each Lambda's individual stats.

Step 10 — Sign Out
What is this

Signing out removes the JWT token from the browser session storage. Anyone opening the same URL after you sign out will see the login screen — not your data. The token cannot be reused once cleared.

What to do

Click Sign out in the top right corner of the navigation bar.

What you see

The page returns to the login screen. No data is visible. The next person to open the URL must authenticate with their own credentials before seeing anything.
